## CRITICAL: Selective Reporting of Correlation Coefficients

**Location:** Manuscript index.qmd:426-428; Notebook Cell 25 annotation box

The manuscript reports:
- LC_Telecast: **Spearman** rho = 0.370, p = 0.037
- SC: **Pearson** r = -0.476, p = 0.006

It switches between Pearson and Spearman, always choosing the test that yields significance. The undisclosed fact:

| Variable | Pearson r | Pearson p | Spearman rho | Spearman p |
|----------|-----------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| LC_Telecast | 0.295 | 0.101 (NOT sig) | 0.370 | 0.037 (sig) |
| SC | -0.476 | 0.006 (sig) | -0.404 | 0.022 (sig) |

Line 426 says "the two cultural dimensions that show statistically significant associations" — but by Pearson, only **one** variable (SC) is significant. LC_Telecast is only significant by Spearman.

**Why Pearson and Spearman diverge for LC_Telecast:** Influence analysis shows that small island territories and northeastern states at the extremes of the NTL distribution (Nagaland, Lakshdweep, Sikkim, Mizoram) exert disproportionate leverage on the Pearson coefficient. Removing any one of these changes Pearson r by 0.05-0.08. Spearman, being rank-based, is robust to these outliers. The Mann-Whitney U test also confirms a significant group difference (U=204, p=0.004).

**Recommended fix:** Option (b) from the audit plan — commit to Spearman consistently for all variables, given the small sample (N=32) and documented outlier sensitivity. State the rationale once and report Spearman throughout. Alternatively, report both coefficients transparently.

## SIGNIFICANT: Multiple Comparisons

**Location:** Cells 12, 25

Six cultural variables tested with both Pearson and Spearman = 12 tests at alpha=0.05. No correction applied.

After correction (12 tests):

| Test | Raw p | Bonferroni | BH-FDR |
|------|-------|-----------|--------|
| SC (Pearson) | 0.006 | 0.070 (marg) | 0.070 (marg) |
| SC (Spearman) | 0.022 | 0.261 | 0.130 |
| LC_Telecast (Spearman) | 0.037 | 0.449 | 0.150 |

Only SC (Pearson) survives at p<0.10 after any correction method.

**Recommended fix:** Since this is an exploratory (not confirmatory) analysis, formal correction may not be required. But the manuscript should acknowledge the multiple testing context, e.g.: "Given the exploratory nature of this analysis across six cultural dimensions, the p-values should be interpreted with appropriate caution."

## MINOR ISSUES

### 1. Confidence Band Uses z=1.96 Instead of t-Distribution (Cell 25)

The 95% CI band uses z=1.96, but with n=32, the correct critical value is t(0.025, 30) = 2.04 — a 4.2% difference. The band is slightly too narrow. Visually negligible but technically imprecise.

**Fix:** Replace `1.96` with `stats.t.ppf(0.975, n-2)`.

### 2. Variable Name: "Per Capita" vs "Per Million" (All cells)

The GEE script computes `NTL / pop_mil` (NTL per million people), not NTL per person. The variable is labeled `ntl_pc` ("per capita"). Since `log(NTL/pop_mil) = log(NTL/pop) + log(1e6)`, the constant shift has zero effect on correlations, regressions, or spatial statistics. All results are mathematically correct.

**Fix:** Add a clarifying note in Cell 1's documentation: "Note: `ntl_pc` is computed as total NTL divided by population in millions; the log transformation makes the scaling irrelevant for all correlation and regression analyses."

### 3. Spatial Weights on Unprojected Coordinates (Cell 14)

KNN distances computed in WGS84 (lat/lon), causing slight distortion at India's latitude range. Comparison with projected coordinates (EPSG:7755) shows 3/32 regions have different 6th-nearest neighbors. Also, island territories (A&N Islands, Lakshdweep) get geographically implausible neighbors:
- A & N Islands' neighbors include Mizoram, Tripura, Manipur — distant mainland states
- Lakshdweep's neighbors include Andhra Pradesh, Pondicherry

This is a known limitation of KNN with island territories but could be noted.

### 4. Tubadji (2025) Citation (Cell 0, index.qmd:422)

The manuscript says `@tubadji_cultural_entropy proposes a Culture-Based Development framework`. The cited paper is "Cultural Entropy, Innovation, and Growth" (Politics & Policy, 2025). The CBD framework was developed over Tubadji's earlier work (book: Edward Elgar 2024, earlier papers from ~2012). The 2025 paper likely applies or extends the framework rather than "proposing" it. Consider citing the book or an earlier foundational paper, or change "proposes" to "advances" or "applies."

## FACTUAL ERRORS FOUND

### 5. NSS 47th Round Dates Are Wrong (Cell 0, Cell 1, Cell 5, index.qmd:423)

The notebook repeatedly states "July 1991 – June 1992" for the NSS 47th Round. Multiple official sources (microdata.gov.in, IHSN catalog, ICSSR data repository) confirm the 47th Round ran **July – December 1991** (6 months only), not a full year.

This error appears in:
- Notebook Cell 0: "NSS 47th Round (July 1991 – June 1992)"
- Notebook Cell 1: variable definitions table
- Notebook Cell 3: code comment
- Notebook Cell 5: "The cultural survey (July 1991 – June 1992)"
- Manuscript index.qmd:423: "NSS 47th Round (1991–92)"

**Impact on analysis:** The temporal alignment claim is still broadly correct (both NTL and NSS data are from ~1991-92), but the specific date range is wrong. The NTL data (1992 annual composite) does NOT overlap with the survey period (July–December 1991) — they are consecutive, not contemporaneous.

**Fix:** Correct to "July – December 1991" everywhere.

### 6. CCNL Citation Year: 2020 vs 2022

The notebook cites "Zhao, Cao, Chen, & Cui, 2020" for the CCNL dataset. The Zenodo deposit date is 2020-09-27, but the accompanying journal paper (Scientific Data, vol. 9, article 424) was published **2022-07-20** (DOI: 10.1038/s41597-022-01540-x).

If citing the journal paper, the correct year is **2022**. If citing the dataset deposit, 2020 is defensible. Should be clarified and made consistent with references.bib.
